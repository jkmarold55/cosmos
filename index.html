Time?

The fastest thing we know is light, and when we determine distance in space, we measure the time it takes for light to reach us from the celestial object we are observing. Here's something crazy to think about: when you look up at the sky at a star, what you see is what the star looked like billions of years ago. For all we know, every star on the Orion belt could undergo complete destruction right now, but we won't see it happen for billions of years!

The Epicenter of Cosmos

    What are we?

    At first glance we live in a great big world with a great many possibility. Sagan argues that although we are tiny beings, insignificant in the face of the greater universe, the connections we have with one another are all part of the fabric of the universe. No known life exists beyond us, and the possiblity that life is the result of coincidence is very likely, so ever step we take, every civilization that rises and falls, every religion established and forgotten, ever great man or woman that is born and dies, all of it is part of a great circle of interconnectedness.
